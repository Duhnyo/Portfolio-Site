About

My Story

I graduated in 2018 with a B.A. in Psychology from UC Santa Barbara and broke into the world of UX by a happy accident when I discovered how frustrated I was trying to understand and learn the software of the software company that I worked at.

Throughout the entire training season as I was learning the ins and outs of the software, the thought that "there must be - there needs to be something better" kept coming to me. 

This led me to explore the different ways that I could address these problems. So I began looking into UX Design and was instantly delighted by how it was a field that looked these issues in the eye and fought them with an elegance that was concerned about the user. I was fascinated by the parallels that UX Design had with psychology and saw it as a way to combine my background in the cognitive sciences with my love for tech and design to create things that would help people.

Now, I'm working on getting my UX Design Certification with CareerFoundry's bootcamp program, specializing in Front-End Web Development, and am interested in opportunities to work as a junior UX/Front-End Designer in the Santa Barbara area, and my dream is to build products that bring tangible value to everyday tech users.
